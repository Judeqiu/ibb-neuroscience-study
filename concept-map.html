IB Neuroscience — Concept Map

Level 1 — Development

Neural Plate → Neural Tube (CNS) + Neural Crest (PNS, melanocytes, facial bones/muscles)

~14,000 genes control brain development | Shh (notochord → ventral/basal plate, motor) | BMP/Wnt (dorsal/alar plate, sensory) | Hox genes pattern hindbrain r2–r11 | Forebrain: Otx2 | Hindbrain: Gbx2

↓
Level 2 — Cells & Tissue Organization

CNS — Brain + Spinal Cord

Nuclei = cell body clusters | Tracts = axon bundles (no connective tissue) | CNS Glia: Astrocytes, Oligodendrocytes, Microglia, Ependyma

Neurons — Structural & Functional Units

Cell body (soma, Nissl bodies = RER) | Dendrites (receive input, dendritic spines) | Axon (conducts AP, no Nissl bodies) | Synapse: presynaptic bouton → synaptic cleft → postsynaptic density

PNS — Nerves + Ganglia

Ganglia = cell body clusters | Nerves = axon bundles + connective tissue sheaths | Schwann cells (myelinate ONE axon segment each)

↓
Level 3 — Myelin & Glial Function

CNS Myelination — Oligodendrocytes

ONE oligodendrocyte → myelin for MANY axons | More glycolipid, less phospholipid | No basal lamina, no endoneurium | CNS axons do NOT regenerate after injury

PNS Myelination — Schwann Cells (Lemmocytes)

ONE Schwann cell → myelin for ONE axon segment | More phospholipid, less glycolipid | Has basal lamina + endoneurium | PNS axons CAN regenerate

Nodes of Ranvier → Saltatory Conduction

Gaps between adjacent myelin segments | Na⁺ channels concentrated at nodes | AP "jumps" node-to-node → 10–100× faster than unmyelinated conduction | Demyelination: 10× slower conduction, may stop entirely

Glial Cells (outnumber neurons 10:1, CAN divide)

Astrocytes (recycle NTs, modulate synapses, K⁺ balance, BBB) | Microglia (CNS immune, bone marrow-derived) | Ependyma (line ventricles, CSF) | Glial tumors: astrocytomas, glioblastomas

↓
Level 4 — Spinal Cord

Spinal Cord Structure

6 functional regions (C1–Co2) | Grey matter: butterfly-shaped, Rexed laminae I–X | Dorsal horn (sensory, laminae 1–4) | Ventral horn (motor, lamina 9 = α-MN) | Lamina 5: pattern generators

Ascending & Descending Tracts

Corticospinal (motor, crosses at pyramidal decussation) | DC-ML (touch/proprioception/vibration, crosses in hindbrain) | Spinothalamic (pain/temp, crosses in spinal cord) | Spinocerebellar (proprioception → cerebellum)

Spinal Nerves & Dermatomes

8 cervical nerves (7 vertebrae) | 12 thoracic | 5 lumbar | 5 sacral | variable coccygeal | Each spinal nerve supplies a specific dermatome (skin strip C1–Co)

↓
Level 5 — Brainstem, Cerebellum & Cranial Nerves

Brainstem = Midbrain + Hindbrain

NOT "midbrain-pons-medulla" (outdated schema!) | CN 3–12 nuclei | Vital centers (breathing, CV) | Corridor for ascending/descending pathways | Reticular formation (consciousness, sleep-wake)

Cerebellum (~80% of all brain neurons!)

3 peduncles: Inferior (spinal cord + olive in), Middle (pontine nuclei in, massive), Superior (output to thalamus) | Ipsilateral body connection (double decussation) | Granule cells = ~70% of all brain neurons

Cranial Nerves (CN 3–12 from brainstem)

Functional classification: SE (somatic efferent), VE (visceral efferent), BE (branchial efferent), SA (somatic afferent), VA (visceral afferent), SS (special sensory) | CN 1 & 2: forebrain, not brainstem

↓
Level 6 — Forebrain (Diencephalon + Telencephalon)

Diencephalon & Hypothalamus

Thalamus: sensory relay (all senses except smell) — VPL, DLG, MG, VL nuclei | Hypothalamus: MOST ROSTRAL part of neural tube (not "under thalamus"!) | Cephalic flexure pushes it ventrally | SCN (circadian clock), VMH (feeding/defense), Mammillary body

Telencephalon — Cortex (180 Brodmann areas)

Neocortex: 6 layers | M1 (BA4, agranular) | S1 (BA3,1,2, granular) | V1 (BA17) | A1 (BA41) | Broca (BA44–45) | Wernicke (BA22) | Hippocampus (memory formation) | Amygdala (fear, social hierarchy)

Basal Ganglia & White Matter

Striatum (caudate + putamen + accumbens) | Pallidum (globus pallidus) | Substantia nigra (dopamine → striatum) | 4 commissures: Corpus callosum, Anterior, Hippocampal, Posterior | Internal capsule: common stroke site

↓
Level 7 — Blood Supply, CSF, Imaging & Examination

Blood Supply — Circle of Willis

ICA + Vertebral → Basilar → Circle of Willis | ACA (medial frontal/parietal, leg area) | MCA (lateral, face/arm/speech; most common stroke) | PCA (occipital, visual cortex) | ACom / PCom: aneurysm sites

CSF Circulation & BBB

CSF: Choroid plexus → Lateral → 3rd → Cerebral aqueduct → 4th → Subarachnoid space → Arachnoid granulations → Superior sagittal sinus | ~500 mL/day, ~150 mL volume, replaced ~3×/day | BBB: tight junctions + basement membrane + astrocyte end-feet

Staining & Imaging Methods

Nissl (RER in soma), Golgi (full neuron, random), Myelin (Luxol fast blue), GFAP (astrocytes), NeuN (neuron nuclei), ChAT (cholinergic neurons) | MRI (soft tissue, MS plaques), CT (acute hemorrhage, bone), EEG (epilepsy), EMG/NCS (peripheral nerve/muscle)

↓
Level 8 — Functional Systems

Motor Systems

Corticospinal tract (voluntary fine distal limb, crosses at pyramidal decussation) | Basal ganglia circuit (select/inhibit movements) | Cerebellum (real-time coordination) | α-Motor neuron = final common pathway (Sherrington) | Extrapyramidal system = discredited term!

Sensory Systems (3-neuron chains)

DC-ML (touch/proprioception/vibration): crosses in HINDBRAIN | Spinothalamic (pain/temp/crude touch): crosses in SPINAL CORD | Visual: retina → LGN → V1 (BA17) | Auditory: cochlea → MGN → A1 (BA41) | Olfactory: bypasses thalamus! → piriform cortex

Autonomic Nervous System (2-neuron chain)

Sympathetic: T1–L2, paravertebral chain, short pre/long post, NA (fight or flight) | Parasympathetic: CN 3,7,9,10 + S2–S4, near-organ ganglia, long pre/short post, ACh (rest & digest) | Enteric NS: 200–600 million gut neurons, independent

Sleep, Memory & Language

Sleep: VLPO switch → inhibits arousal centers (LC/NA, Raphe/5-HT, Tegmental/ACh, TMN/Histamine) | 90-min cycles | Memory: Semantic (facts) vs Episodic (personal, H.M. case) | Hippocampus = formation; Cortex = long-term storage | Aphasia: Wernicke receptive (BA22) vs Broca expressive (BA44–45)

↓
Level 9 — Clinical Disorders

Stroke

MCA most common | Internal capsule vulnerable | "Time is brain" — ~1.9M neurons lost per minute | Ischemic (80%) vs Hemorrhagic

Parkinson's Disease

Substantia nigra pars compacta degeneration | Resting tremor (disappears with movement) | Rigidity, Bradykinesia | L-DOPA | DBS: subthalamic nucleus, zona incerta

Multiple Sclerosis

Autoimmune attack on oligodendrocytes → CNS demyelination | Relapsing-remitting | MRI: plaques | CSF: oligoclonal bands | First episode often optic neuritis

Alzheimer's Disease

Most common dementia (~65%) | Begins in hippocampus → memory loss | Aβ plaques (extracellular) | Neurofibrillary tangles (hyperphosphorylated tau, intracellular) | Death ~4 years from diagnosis

Epilepsy

Cortical hyperexcitability → seizures | EEG: primary diagnostic tool | Generalized (tonic-clonic/grand mal) vs Absence (petit mal) | Temporal lobe epilepsy: olfactory hallucinations, déjà vu

Spastic vs Flaccid Paralysis

Spastic (CST damage): ↑tone, ↑reflexes, Babinski (+), minimal atrophy | Flaccid (MN damage): ↓tone, ↓reflexes, Babinski (−), marked atrophy, fasciculations

Meningitis / Poliomyelitis

Meningitis: fever, headache, neck stiffness, ↑WBC in CSF | Bacterial treatable (antibiotics), viral supportive | Polio: virus → motor neurons via retrograde axonal transport → flaccid paralysis

ALS (Motor Neuron Disease)

UMN + LMN degeneration | Progressive weakness → respiratory failure | Fatal within ~3 years | "Amyotrophic" = LMN (muscle wasting) | "Lateral sclerosis" = UMN (corticospinal tract gliosis)

Huntington's Disease

Autosomal dominant | CAG trinucleotide repeat in HTT gene | Degeneration of GABAergic medium spiny neurons in striatum | Chorea, cognitive decline, psychiatric symptoms | Onset 35–50 years

Cerebral Palsy

Non-progressive movement disorder from developmental brain damage | 4 subtypes: Spastic (~70%, CST), Ataxic (cerebellum), Athetoid/Dyskinetic (basal ganglia), Mixed

Schizophrenia / Depression / Bipolar

Schizophrenia: 1% population, dopamine/serotonin, auditory hallucinations | Depression: 1/3 lifetime prevalence, SSRIs, CBT | Bipolar: manic + depressive episodes, lithium, sodium valproate

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Thiamine (B1) deficiency → mostly chronic alcohol abuse | Wernicke encephalopathy: confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia | Korsakoff: anterograde amnesia, confabulation, mammillary body atrophy | ~20% mortality within weeks

CJD / Prion Disease

Misfolded PrPsc protein → rapidly progressive dementia | Sporadic: 1 in 1,000,000 | Variant CJD: from BSE ("mad cow disease") contaminated beef | Death within 6–12 months of symptom onset

ADHD / Autism / OCD / Tourette

ADHD: inattention, hyperactivity, methylphenidate | Autism: social/communication, NOT caused by vaccines (Wakefield retracted) | OCD: SSRIs reduce symptoms in ~60% | Tourette: motor + vocal tics

Spina Bifida / FAS / Rubella

Neural tube defects: folate deficiency → spina bifida (preventable with supplementation) | Fetal alcohol syndrome: avoid alcohol in first 8 weeks | Rubella: deafness, blindness, brain abnormalities → vaccination

Myasthenia Gravis / Peripheral Neuropathy

Myasthenia gravis: autoimmune, antibodies block postsynaptic ACh receptors → exercise-dependent muscle weakness | Peripheral neuropathy: diabetes/alcohol → longest nerves first (glove-and-stocking numbness)

Core Principle: Histological Structure → Anatomical Organization → Functional Circuitry → Clinical Signs When Disrupted
