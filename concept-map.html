IB Neuroscience — Concept Map

Level 1 — Development

Neural Plate → Neural Tube (CNS) + Neural Crest (PNS, melanocytes, facial bones/muscles)

~14,000 genes control brain development | Shh (notochord → ventral/basal plate, motor) | BMP/Wnt (dorsal/alar plate, sensory) | Hox genes pattern hindbrain r2–r11 | Forebrain: Otx2 | Hindbrain: Gbx2

Level 2 — Cells & Tissue Organization

CNS — Brain + Spinal Cord

Nuclei = cell body clusters | Tracts = axon bundles (no connective tissue) | CNS Glia: Astrocytes, Oligodendrocytes, Microglia, Ependyma

Neurons — Structural & Functional Units

Cell body (soma, Nissl bodies = RER) | Dendrites (receive input, spines) | Axon (conducts AP, no Nissl) | Synapse: presynaptic bouton → cleft → postsynaptic density

PNS — Nerves + Ganglia

Ganglia = cell body clusters | Nerves = axon bundles + connective tissue sheaths | Schwann cells (myelinate ONE axon segment each) | Cranial & spinal nerves

Level 3 — Myelin & Glial Function

CNS Myelination — Oligodendrocytes

ONE oligodendrocyte → myelin for MANY axons | More glycolipid | No basal lamina, no endoneurium | CNS axons do NOT regenerate after injury

PNS Myelination — Schwann Cells (Lemmocytes)

ONE Schwann cell → one axon segment | More phospholipid | Has basal lamina + endoneurium | PNS axons CAN regenerate

Nodes of Ranvier → Saltatory Conduction

Gaps between myelin segments | Na⁺ channels concentrated at nodes | AP "jumps" node-to-node, 10–100× faster | Demyelination: 10× slower, may stop entirely (MS, Guillain-Barré)

Glial Cells (outnumber neurons 10:1, CAN divide)

Astrocytes (recycle NTs, modulate synapses, K⁺, BBB) | Microglia (CNS immune) | Ependyma (line ventricles, CSF) | Tumors: astrocytomas, glioblastomas

Level 4 — Spinal Cord

Spinal Cord Structure

6 functional regions (C1–Co2) | Grey matter: butterfly-shaped, Rexed laminae I–X | Dorsal horn (sensory, laminae 1–4) | Ventral horn (motor, lamina 9 = α-MN) | Lamina 5: pattern generators

Ascending & Descending Tracts

Corticospinal (motor, crosses at pyramidal decussation) | DC-ML (touch/proprioception/vibration, crosses in HINDBRAIN) | Spinothalamic (pain/temp, crosses in SPINAL CORD) | Spinocerebellar (proprioception → cerebellum)

Spinal Nerves & Dermatomes

8 cervical nerves (7 vertebrae) | 12 thoracic | 5 lumbar | 5 sacral | Each spinal nerve supplies a specific dermatome (skin strip C1–Co)

Level 5 — Brainstem, Cerebellum & Cranial Nerves

Brainstem = Midbrain + Hindbrain

NOT "midbrain-pons-medulla" (outdated!) | CN 3–12 nuclei | Vital centers (breathing, CV) | Corridor for ascending/descending pathways | Reticular formation (consciousness)

Cerebellum (~80% of all neurons)

3 peduncles: Inferior (spinal cord + olive in), Middle (pontine nuclei in), Superior (output to thalamus) | Ipsilateral body connection (double decussation) | Granule cells = ~70% of all brain neurons | Coordinates movement in real-time

Cranial Nerves (CN 3–12 from brainstem)

Functional classification: SE, VE, BE, SA, VA, SS | CN 1 (Olfactory) & CN 2 (Optic) from forebrain | Precerebellar nuclei: Inferior olive (climbing fibers), Pontine nuclei (mossy fibers)

Level 6 — Forebrain (Diencephalon + Telencephalon)

Diencephalon & Hypothalamus

Thalamus: sensory relay (all senses except smell) — VPL, DLG, MG, VL nuclei | Hypothalamus: MOST ROSTRAL neural tube (cephalic flexure pushes it ventrally) | SCN (circadian clock), VMH, Mammillary body | Posterior pituitary: oxytocin, ADH

Telencephalon — Cortex & Deep Structures

Neocortex: 6 layers, ~180 Brodmann areas | M1 (BA4), S1 (BA3,1,2), V1 (BA17), A1 (BA41), Broca (44–45), Wernicke (22) | Hippocampus (memory formation) | Amygdala (fear, social hierarchy) | Striatum + Pallidum (basal ganglia)

White Matter & Commissures

4 commissures: Corpus callosum (largest), Anterior, Hippocampal, Posterior | Projection fibers (internal capsule — common stroke site) | Association fibers | Limbic system = discredited term!

Level 7 — Blood Supply, CSF, Imaging & Examination

Blood Supply — Circle of Willis

ICA + Vertebral arteries → Basilar → Circle of Willis | ACA (medial frontal/parietal, leg area) | MCA (lateral, face/arm/speech, most common stroke) | PCA (occipital, visual cortex) | Anterior/Posterior communicating: aneurysm sites

CSF Circulation & Blood-Brain Barrier

CSF: Choroid plexus → Lateral → 3rd → Cerebral aqueduct → 4th → Subarachnoid space → Arachnoid granulations → Superior sagittal sinus | ~500 mL/day, replaced ~3×/day | BBB: tight junctions + basement membrane + astrocytic end-feet

Staining & Imaging Methods

Histology: Nissl (RER), Golgi (full neuron), Myelin (Luxol fast blue), GFAP (astrocytes), NeuN, ChAT | Imaging: MRI (soft tissue, MS plaques), CT (acute hemorrhage, bone), EEG (epilepsy), EMG/NCS (peripheral nerve/muscle), Lumbar puncture (CSF analysis)

Level 8 — Functional Systems

Motor Systems

Corticospinal tract (voluntary fine distal limb, crosses at pyramidal decussation) | Basal ganglia circuit (select/inhibit movements) | Cerebellum (real-time coordination) | α-Motor neuron = final common pathway (Sherrington) | Extrapyramidal system = discredited!

Sensory Systems (3-neuron chains)

DC-ML (touch/proprioception/vibration): crosses in HINDBRAIN | Spinothalamic (pain/temp): crosses in SPINAL CORD | Visual: retina → LGN → V1 (BA17) | Auditory: cochlea → MGN → A1 (BA41) | Olfactory: bypasses thalamus! → piriform cortex | Vestibular: balance & eye movements

Autonomic Nervous System (2-neuron chain)

Sympathetic: T1–L2, paravertebral chain, short pre/long post, NA (fight or flight) | Parasympathetic: CN 3,7,9,10 + S2–S4, near-organ ganglia, long pre/short post, ACh (rest & digest) | Enteric NS: 200–600 million gut neurons, independent local reflexes

Sleep, Memory & Language

Sleep: VLPO switch inhibits arousal centers (LC/NA, Raphe/5-HT, Tegmental/ACh, TMN/Histamine), 90-min cycles | Memory: Semantic (facts) vs Episodic (personal, H.M. case), Hippocampus = formation | Aphasia: Wernicke receptive (BA22) vs Broca expressive (BA44–45)

Level 9 — Clinical Disorders (structure determines dysfunction)

Stroke

MCA most common | Internal capsule vulnerable | "Time is brain" — ~1.9M neurons lost per minute | Ischemic (80%) vs Hemorrhagic | Contralateral symptoms

Parkinson's Disease

Substantia nigra pars compacta degeneration → dopamine loss | Resting tremor (disappears with movement) | Rigidity, Bradykinesia, Postural instability | L-DOPA treatment | DBS: subthalamic nucleus, zona incerta

Multiple Sclerosis

Autoimmune attack on oligodendrocytes → CNS demyelination | Relapsing-remitting course | MRI: plaques | CSF: oligoclonal bands | First episode often optic neuritis (blurred vision)

Alzheimer's Disease

Most common dementia (~65% of cases) | Begins in hippocampus → early memory loss | Aβ plaques (extracellular) + Neurofibrillary tangles (hyperphosphorylated tau, intracellular) | Death ~4 years from diagnosis (infections)

Epilepsy

Cortical hyperexcitability → recurrent seizures | EEG: primary diagnostic tool | Generalized (tonic-clonic/grand mal) vs Absence (petit mal) | Temporal lobe epilepsy: olfactory hallucinations, déjà vu, hippocampal/amygdala involvement

Spastic vs Flaccid Paralysis

Spastic (CST/UMN damage): ↑tone, ↑reflexes, Babinski (+), minimal atrophy | Flaccid (LMN damage): ↓tone, ↓reflexes, Babinski (−), marked atrophy, fasciculations | Key clinical distinction for lesion localization

Meningitis / Poliomyelitis

Meningitis: fever, headache, neck stiffness, ↑WBC in CSF, bacterial (antibiotics) vs viral | Polio: virus enters via neuromuscular junction → retrograde axonal transport → motor neuron death → flaccid paralysis | Nearly eradicated by vaccination

ALS (Motor Neuron Disease)

UMN + LMN degeneration | "Amyotrophic" = LMN loss (muscle wasting) | "Lateral sclerosis" = UMN loss (corticospinal tract gliosis) | Progressive weakness → respiratory failure → fatal within ~3 years

Huntington's Disease

Autosomal dominant | CAG trinucleotide repeat expansion in HTT gene on chromosome 4 | Degeneration of GABAergic medium spiny neurons in striatum | Chorea, cognitive decline, psychiatric symptoms | Onset 35–50 years, inversely correlated with CAG repeat length

Cerebral Palsy

Non-progressive movement disorder from damage to developing brain (prenatal/perinatal/early postnatal) | 4 subtypes: Spastic (~70%, CST damage), Ataxic (cerebellar), Athetoid/Dyskinetic (basal ganglia), Mixed | No cure; maximize independence

Schizophrenia / Depression / Bipolar

Schizophrenia: 1% population, dopamine/serotonin, auditory hallucinations, multifactorial | Depression: 1/3 lifetime prevalence, SSRIs, CBT | Bipolar: manic + depressive episodes, lithium, sodium valproate — distinguish from unipolar depression!

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Thiamine (B1) deficiency → mostly chronic alcohol abuse | Wernicke encephalopathy: confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia (reversible if treated early) | Korsakoff: anterograde amnesia, confabulation, mammillary body atrophy | ~20% mortality within weeks

CJD / Prion Disease

Misfolded PrPsc protein induces normal PrPc to misfold → rapidly progressive dementia, myoclonus, ataxia | Sporadic: 1 in 1,000,000 | Variant CJD: from BSE-contaminated beef | Death within 6–12 months of symptom onset | No treatment available

ADHD / Autism / OCD / Tourette

ADHD: inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity → methylphenidate | Autism: social/communication deficits, repetitive behaviors — NOT caused by vaccines (Wakefield retracted, banned) | OCD: obsessions + compulsions, SSRIs reduce symptoms in ~60% | Tourette: motor + vocal tics

Spina Bifida / FAS / Rubella

Neural tube defects: folate deficiency → spina bifida (preventable with preconception supplementation) | Fetal alcohol syndrome: avoid alcohol in first 8 weeks of pregnancy | Rubella (German measles): deafness, blindness, brain abnormalities → vaccination prevents

Myasthenia Gravis / Peripheral Neuropathy

Myasthenia gravis: autoimmune, antibodies block postsynaptic ACh receptors at NMJ → exercise-dependent muscle weakness, treatment: immunosuppressants + thymectomy | Peripheral neuropathy: diabetes/alcohol → longest nerves first (glove-and-stocking distribution numbness)

Core Principle: Histological Structure → Anatomical Organization → Functional Circuitry → Clinical Signs When Disrupted
